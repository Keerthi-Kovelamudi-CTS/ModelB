KEY INSIGHTS:

At 12 months before diagnosis, classical bladder cancer
symptoms that are in NICE guidelines are largely absent from GP records:

  • Haematuria:   5.7% of cancer patients (94.3% have NONE)
  • Dysuria:      1.9% of cancer patients (98.1% have NONE)
  • LUTS:         0.5% of cancer patients (99.5% have NONE)
  • Raised WBC:   0.2% of cancer patients (99.8% have NONE)

Combined: fewer than 8% of cancer patients show ANY
classical urinary symptom at the 12-month window.

Yet the model achieves AUC 0.846 and sensitivity 80.1%.

This means the model catches 80% of cancer patients
despite >92% of them having NO major CODES recorded urinary symptoms,
using INDIRECT signals besides major urinary symptoms:

  • Smoking history and intensity patterns
  • Haemoglobin trends (subtle anaemia before symptoms)
  • eGFR changes (kidney function shifts)
  • Healthcare utilisation patterns (GP visit frequency)
  • BMI variability and metabolic markers
  • Medication prescribing patterns, etc.

As prediction window shortens, symptoms emerge and
supplement these indirect signals:

  3mo window:  15.2% haematuria, 3.0% dysuria
               → Model uses BOTH symptoms + risk factors
               → AUC 0.880

  6mo window:   8.6% haematuria, 2.4% dysuria
               → Symptoms fading, risk factors dominate
               → AUC 0.853

  12mo window:  5.7% haematuria, 1.9% dysuria
               → Almost no symptoms present
               → Risk factors + lab trends ONLY
               → AUC 0.846

The total AUC drop from 3mo to 12mo is only 0.034,
despite a 63% reduction in haematuria prevalence and
a 37% reduction in dysuria prevalence. This demonstrates
the model's robustness: it retains 96% of its
discriminative performance even when the strongest
clinical features are absent.

Note: Raised WBC shows NO discriminative value at any
window (ratio 0.7-1.0x), confirming that bladder cancer
— unlike haematological malignancies — does not produce
systemic inflammatory markers detectable in routine
blood tests at the pre-diagnostic stage.

LUTS loses ALL discriminative value at 12 months
(ratio 0.8x — actually MORE common in non-cancer),
consistent with LUTS being driven by benign prostatic
hyperplasia rather than malignancy.
